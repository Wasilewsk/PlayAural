\*\*Backgammon\*\*

Backgammon is one of the oldest racing board games in the world. Two players guide fifteen checkers each around a shared track of twenty-four points, trying to bring all of their checkers home and bear them off before the opponent can do the same.

\*\*Gameplay\*\*

Each game begins with an opening roll. Both players roll one die, and the higher roll goes first. Those two opening numbers are then used immediately as that player's first turn.

After that, players take turns rolling two dice and moving their checkers according to the numbers rolled. Each die is a separate move. If the dice are different, you normally must use both if legal moves exist. If you roll doubles, you play that number four times.

The turn menu presents the legal moves available at that moment. You continue choosing moves until all usable dice have been spent or no legal move remains. If only one die can legally be played, the higher die must be used.

\*\*Movement and Hitting\*\*

You may move onto:

\* An empty point.
\* A point occupied by your own checkers.
\* A point occupied by exactly one opposing checker.

If you land on a point with exactly one opposing checker, that checker is hit and sent to the bar.

You may not move onto a point occupied by two or more opposing checkers. Those points are blocked.

If you have any checker on the bar, you must re-enter that checker before moving any others. A checker on the bar re-enters using the dice just like any other move, but it must enter on an open point.

\*\*Bearing Off and Scoring\*\*

Once all fifteen of your checkers are in your home board, you may begin bearing them off. An exact roll may bear off a checker directly. If you roll higher than needed, you may only bear off from your furthest checker when no checker remains farther away.

The winner of a game scores match points:

\* \*\*1 point\*\* for a normal win, if the loser has already borne off at least one checker.
\* \*\*2 points\*\* for a gammon, if the loser has borne off none.
\* \*\*3 points\*\* for a backgammon, if the loser has borne off none and still has a checker on the bar or in the winner's home board.

If the doubling cube has been used, the final value of the cube multiplies the score for that game.

\*\*Doubling Cube and Crawford Rule\*\*

In matches longer than one point, the active player may offer a double before rolling. If the opponent accepts, the cube value doubles and ownership of the cube passes to the accepting player. If the opponent drops, the offering player wins the current game immediately for the cube's current value.

The Crawford rule is applied automatically. When a player is exactly one point away from winning the match, the next game becomes a Crawford game and the doubling cube is disabled for that game.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

\* \*\*Match Length:\*\* Default 5. Range 1 to 25. This is the number of points needed to win the match.
\* \*\*Bot Strategy:\*\* Default Simple. Choices: Simple, Smart, or Random.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*R:\*\* Roll the dice.
\* \*\*Shift+D:\*\* Offer a double before rolling.
\* \*\*Y:\*\* Accept an offered double.
\* \*\*N:\*\* Drop an offered double.
\* \*\*U:\*\* Undo the last sub-move of the current turn.
\* \*\*V:\*\* Read the full board.
\* \*\*E:\*\* Read the checker counts on the bar and already borne off.
\* \*\*P:\*\* Read the pip counts for both players.
\* \*\*C:\*\* Read the doubling cube status.
\* \*\*X:\*\* Read the remaining dice for the current turn.
