\*\*Dominos\*\*



Dominos is PlayAural's classic line-building domino game. It supports 2 to 4 players, optional 2v2 teams, Double-6 and Double-9 sets, Draw and Block rules, configurable opening rules, optional four-way spinner openings, and standard leftover-pip scoring.



\*\*Gameplay\*\*



Each round begins with a shuffled domino set. In a Double-6 game, each player receives 7 tiles in a 2-player match or 5 tiles in a 3- or 4-player match. In a Double-9 game, each player receives 10 tiles in a 2-player match or 7 tiles in a 3- or 4-player match. All undealt tiles form the boneyard.



One opening tile is placed automatically according to the selected opening rule. After that, players take turns adding one tile at a time to the open ends of the chain by matching numbers.



If the opening tile is a double and spinner play is enabled, that opening double becomes the center spinner. The chain can then grow in four directions: left, right, up, and down. Otherwise, play stays on the normal two ends of the line.



The first player to empty their hand wins the round. If nobody can continue, the round becomes blocked and is scored from the tiles still held by each side.



\*\*Opening The Round\*\*



The host can choose one of these opening rules:



\* \*\*Highest double:\*\* The highest double held by any player opens the round.

\* \*\*Highest tile:\*\* The tile with the highest pip total opens the round.

\* \*\*Highest set double:\*\* The maximum double of the chosen set opens if someone holds it. If not, the game falls back to Highest double.

\* \*\*Random player:\*\* A random player opens with the highest tile in their hand.

\* \*\*Previous round winner:\*\* The player who won the previous round opens with the highest tile in their hand.



\*\*Drawing And Knocking\*\*



On your turn, if you have a legal play, you must play a tile. You cannot choose to draw or knock instead.



\* \*\*Draw mode:\*\* If you have no legal play, you draw from the boneyard until you either find a playable tile or the boneyard runs out. If you draw a playable tile, it is played immediately. If the boneyard becomes empty and you still cannot play, you knock and your turn ends.

\* \*\*Block mode:\*\* There is no drawing. If you cannot play, you knock immediately.



A round is treated as blocked as soon as the boneyard is empty and no player has a legal move. This prevents endless pass loops.



\*\*Blocked Rounds\*\*



When a round is blocked, the winner is determined by the lowest total pip value still held:



\* \*\*Individual mode:\*\* The player with the lowest pip total wins the round.

\* \*\*2v2 mode:\*\* The team with the lowest combined pip total wins the round.



If the lowest total is tied, the blocked round scores no points and a new round begins after a short pause.



\*\*Scoring\*\*



If a player empties their hand, that player or team scores the total pip value remaining in all opposing hands.



If a round is blocked, the winning player or team also scores the total pip value remaining in all opposing hands.



The match ends when one side reaches the target score and is the sole leader. If multiple sides are tied at or above the target score, play continues until the tie is broken.



\*\*Customizable Options\*\*



The host can adjust the following settings before the match starts:



\* \*\*Target Score:\*\* The score needed to win the match. Default: 100. Range: 20 to 500.

\* \*\*Mode:\*\* Choose Draw or Block rules. Default: Draw.

\* \*\*Domino Set:\*\* Choose Double-6 or Double-9. Default: Double-6.

\* \*\*Spinner:\*\* Controls whether an opening double creates a four-way spinner. Default: On.

\* \*\*Opening Rule:\*\* Chooses how each round starts. Default: Highest double.

\* \*\*Team Mode:\*\* Play individually, or use 2v2 teams when four players are seated.



\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*



\* \*\*Space:\*\* Draw from the boneyard.

\* \*\*P:\*\* Knock when you cannot play.

\* \*\*V:\*\* Open the full chain view.

\* \*\*C:\*\* Read the exposed end values.

\* \*\*E:\*\* Read how many tiles the other players are holding.

\* \*\*W:\*\* Read your hand.
