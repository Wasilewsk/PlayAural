\*\*Citadels\*\*

Citadels is a hidden-role city-building card game for 4 to 8 players. Your main goal is to complete your city by being the first player to build 7 districts. After that final round ends, the player with the highest score wins.

\*\*Gameplay\*\*

Every player starts the game with 2 gold and 4 district cards.

In this game, \*\*completing a city\*\* means building 7 districts in your city.

Each round works like this:

1\. \*\*Choose characters:\*\* The player with the crown chooses first, then the choice passes around the table. Depending on the number of players, some character cards are removed before the draft, so not every character appears every round.

2\. \*\*Call characters in rank order:\*\* After everyone has chosen, the game calls the characters from rank 1 upward. If you chose the called character and it was not assassinated, you reveal it and take your turn.

3\. \*\*Collect resources:\*\* On your turn, choose exactly one of these:

\* Take 2 gold.

\* Draw 2 district cards and keep 1.

4\. \*\*Use abilities:\*\* After taking resources, you may use any optional character ability or special district ability that is available to you.

5\. \*\*Build districts:\*\* You may then build districts in your city. Most characters may build 1 district each turn. The Architect may build up to 3.

\* You pay the printed gold cost to build a district unless another effect changes the cost.

\* You normally cannot build a second district with the same name in your city.

\* If you have Quarry, you are allowed to build duplicate district names.

The next rank is then called, and play continues until the round ends.

\*\*Special Mechanics\*\*

\* \*\*Characters in this game:\*\* Assassin, Thief, Magician, King, Bishop, Merchant, Architect, and Warlord are always used. In 8-player games, the Queen is also added.

\* \*\*Assassin:\*\* Chooses one character rank. That rank loses its turn completely when called later in the round.

\* \*\*Thief:\*\* Chooses one character rank to rob. The Thief cannot target the Assassin, cannot target the Thief, and cannot target the rank that was assassinated. When the chosen rank is revealed, the Thief steals all of that player's gold.

\* \*\*Magician:\*\* May either swap their whole hand with another player, or discard any number of cards and draw the same number of replacements.

\* \*\*King:\*\* Gains 1 gold for each noble district in their city and takes the crown. If the King was assassinated, the player who chose the King still takes the crown at the end of the round.

\* \*\*Bishop:\*\* Gains 1 gold for each religious district in their city. While the Bishop is active this round, the Warlord cannot destroy that player's districts.

\* \*\*Merchant:\*\* Gains 1 gold for each trade district in their city, plus 1 extra gold.

\* \*\*Architect:\*\* Immediately draws 2 extra district cards and may build up to 3 districts this turn.

\* \*\*Warlord:\*\* Gains 1 gold for each military district in their city. The Warlord may destroy 1 district by paying that district's cost minus 1 gold. The Warlord cannot destroy districts in a completed city, cannot destroy a protected Bishop city this round, and cannot destroy the Keep.

\* \*\*Queen:\*\* Used only in 8-player games. If the Queen is seated next to the King, the Queen gains 3 gold.

\* \*\*Special districts in this game:\*\*

\* \*\*Dragon Gate:\*\* Worth 2 extra points at the end of the game.

\* \*\*Factory:\*\* Your other unique districts cost 1 less gold to build.

\* \*\*Haunted Quarter:\*\* At the end of the game, it may count as any one district type you need for the five-type bonus.

\* \*\*Imperial Treasury:\*\* Worth 1 point for each gold you still have at the end of the game.

\* \*\*Keep:\*\* Cannot be destroyed by the Warlord.

\* \*\*Laboratory:\*\* Once per turn, discard 1 card from your hand to gain 2 gold.

\* \*\*Library:\*\* If you choose the draw-cards resource action, you keep both drawn cards instead of keeping only 1.

\* \*\*Map Room:\*\* Worth 1 point for each card left in your hand at the end of the game.

\* \*\*Quarry:\*\* Lets you build districts with duplicate names in your city.

\* \*\*School of Magic:\*\* When the King, Bishop, Merchant, or Warlord collects district income, this district may count as the district type you need.

\* \*\*Smithy:\*\* Once per turn after taking resources, pay 2 gold to draw 3 cards.

\* \*\*Statue:\*\* Worth 5 extra points if you hold the crown at the end of the game.

\* \*\*Thieves' Den:\*\* When building it, you may discard cards from your hand to help pay the cost. Each discarded card pays for 1 gold.

\* \*\*Wishing Well:\*\* Worth 1 extra point for each unique district in your city, including itself.

\*\*Scoring\*\*

The game enters its final round as soon as at least one player has built 7 districts. Everyone still finishes that round, then the scores are counted.

You score:

\* The total printed cost of every district in your city.

\* \+3 points if your city contains all 5 district types.

\* \+4 points if you were the first player to complete a city.

\* \+2 points if you also completed a city during the same final round, but were not first.

\* Any extra points from special districts.

If players are tied on score, the tied player who revealed the highest-ranked character in the final round wins the tie.

\*\*Customizable Options\*\*

Citadels does not use any game-specific table setup options. The settings screen is intentionally empty for this game.

\*\*Keyboard Shortcuts\*\*

\* \*\*Space:\*\* Draw district cards.

\* \*\*G:\*\* Take 2 gold.

\* \*\*H:\*\* Read your hand.

\* \*\*V:\*\* Read all cities at the table.

\* \*\*I:\*\* Read the short status summary.

\* \*\*Shift+I:\*\* Open the detailed status view.

\* \*\*K:\*\* Read your current hidden character.

\* \*\*F:\*\* Read the faceup discarded characters.
